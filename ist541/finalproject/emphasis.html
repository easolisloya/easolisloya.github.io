Explanation and Examples

Explanation:
The Emphasis principle of design, often also called Dominance,Â focuses on making a particular element or area of a design stand out to create a focal point and guide the viewer's eye through the composition.

Examples:
Artists and designers achieve emphasis through various techniques, such as creating contrast in color, size, texture, or value, using isolation, or placing the emphasized element in a strategic position within the design to ensure it's the first thing the viewer notices

warning Be careful!
Try not to overemphasize too many elements at one time, as this could create or increase cognitive load.
